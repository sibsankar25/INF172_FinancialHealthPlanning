Design Process

3 rounds of user research & 3 rounds of design iterations

Problem Statement

How might we design a simulation game that helps young adults understand the benefits of having an HSA account and the importance of financial planning?

Competitor Analysis

Solution Type Strengths Weaknesses Cost Target Users
HSA Expert Certificate Course America's Health Insurance Plans (AHIP) Certification Online certification course HSA knowledge depth, industry credibility, structured learning modules High cost, short duration (2.5 hrs), not engaging ~$795–$1,005 Professionals seeking in-depth HSA knowledge
HSAnswers Health Equity AI Chatbot AI Chatbot Easy access to HSA questions, convenient, quick answers Limited engagement, passive learning only Free General public seeking HSA-related knowledge
Fidelity Fidelity Resource Platform Resource platform Handful of useful HSA resources and materials for consumers Limited engagement, no interactivity Free Consumers interested in HSA-related knowledge
HSA Life Path Our Team Our Project Educational game on HSAs Engaging, interactive simulated real-life HSA application Could be improved with more in-game resources Free Young adults interested in HSA

Persona

We created a user persona to reflect on our user needs and requirements. Our character's name is Daniel. He is a current college student in his 2nd year of studies. He wants to learn more about HSA and related products. However, he finds current resources to be overwhelming and lack clear guidance on information related to HSA. We address his needs by creating a game.

Limitations & Next Steps

What we couldn't do yet — and where we want to take this next.

Current Limitations

  • Small sample size (n = 26 max) — findings are preliminary
  • Convenience sample may not generalize to all undergrads
  • HSA/no-HSA comparison tracker was not implemented
  • Prototype is not yet a fully functional game build
